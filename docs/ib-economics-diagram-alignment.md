## 2. What the sources say (concise paraphrase)

- The guide's exam guidance states that students are expected, **where appropriate**, to include
  **correctly labelled and clearly drawn** diagrams, and that **some individual questions
  specify that a diagram is essential** (S1, p. 59). Diagrams are therefore contextual, not a
  universal requirement of every answer.
- AO4 expects students to "produce and use diagrams to help explain economic theory, concepts
  and real-world issues" (S1). The syllabus prescribes an explicit list of diagrams per topic
  (e.g. "Diagram: market equilibrium", "Diagram: Lorenz curve…", "Diagram: showing the effect
  of a tariff on price, production, consumption, expenditures, revenues, welfare") — what a
  diagram must show is defined topic-by-topic and question-by-question, not generically.
- 4-mark "using a diagram, explain…" questions are marked on a two-level scale (S2, several
  questions, verbatim structure): Level 1 (1–2 marks): "There is a correct diagram **OR** an
  accurate written response." Level 2 (3–4 marks): "There is a correct diagram **AND** an
  accurate written response."
- "**Candidates who incorrectly label diagrams can be awarded a maximum of [3]**" on
  4-markers (and "[2]" on a 3-marker) (S2) — mislabelled diagrams cap below full marks.
- Labelling accepts standard conventions and abbreviations, stated per diagram family, e.g.
  "the vertical axis should be **price or p**. The horizontal axis should be **quantity or
  q**" (demand & supply, S2); "Any abbreviation of the previous terms is acceptable" (AD/AS,
  S2). Every such note ends "**A title is not necessary.**" (S2, S3).
- Markschemes explicitly reward **alternative correct constructions** (e.g. an exchange-rate
  answer drawn as depreciation-then-restoration "can be fully rewarded"; PPCs "could be linear
  or curved… intersect or be non-intersecting"; even reversed country labels acceptable) (S2).

## 3. Verified vs question-specific vs never-assume

**Verified general principles (safe for Aptly to rely on):**
1. The 1–2 = diagram OR explanation / 3–4 = diagram AND explanation structure for 4-mark
   "using a diagram" questions (S2 — consistent across every such question in the paper).
2. Incorrect labelling caps below full marks (S2, S3).
3. Titles are never required ("A title is not necessary" appears in every diagram note; S2, S3).
4. Standard abbreviations are acceptable (p/q, price level/real Y variants, i/r, etc.; S2, S3).
5. Diagrams in longer responses are "where appropriate", not universal (S1).
6. Multiple correct constructions can earn full credit (S2).

**Question-specific (Aptly must read from the question, never generalise):**
- The exact required content of a diagram (which curves, which shift, which areas/effects must
  be indicated) is enumerated per question in the markscheme (e.g. "three requirements:
  correctly labelled demand and supply diagram; a shift of the demand curve to the right;
  effect on revenue correctly indicated" — S2).
- Whether a diagram is essential at all for a given question (S1).

**Unverified assumptions Aptly must not make:**
- That any specific label format is the single correct one.
- That equilibrium points, shift arrows, or welfare areas are required on diagram types or
  questions where they are not relevant.
- That a numeric mark can be derived from a photo: markschemes judge "correct diagram" against
  question-specific requirements Aptly cannot reliably enumerate for arbitrary pasted questions.
- That examiner-facing notes generalise across sessions — each markscheme governs its paper.

## 4. Diagram-family table

| Family | Commonly expected (evidence) | Must remain question-specific | Aptly must never claim universally mandatory |
|---|---|---|---|
| Demand & supply | Axes price/p vs quantity/q; curves labelled; relevant shift shown (S2) | Which curve shifts, direction, and required indicated effects (e.g. revenue) | A title; D/S written out in full; any single label style |
| AD/AS | Axes price level vs real output/real GDP/real Y ("any abbreviation… acceptable"); AD/AS labelled; relevant shift (S2) | Which curve shifts; Keynesian vs monetarist LRAS shape depends on the question (S1) | A title; one particular AS shape; equilibrium annotation style |
| PPC | Two axes as the two goods/categories; curve(s) drawn; opportunity-cost logic visible (S1, S2) | Linear vs curved; one or two PPCs; which movement/shift matters (S2 accepts many forms) | Equilibrium points or shift arrows (no market equilibrium exists on a PPC); a title |
| Exchange rate | Axes exchange rate (several accepted phrasings) vs quantity of the currency; relevant shift (S2) | Which curve shifts; appreciation vs depreciation direction; alternative constructions fully rewarded (S2) | A title; a single axis phrasing; one fixed construction |
| Lorenz curve | Line of equality plus Lorenz curve(s); cumulative-share axes (S1, S2) | Which curve movement (toward/away from equality) the question needs | Equilibrium, shift arrows, or welfare areas (not applicable); a title |
| Externalities | MSB/MPB or MSC/MPC divergence; welfare loss/gain area where asked; y-axis may be price and/or costs/benefits (S3) | Consumption vs production case; whether the welfare area must be shaded; S and D labels "not required" (S3) | That S/D labels are compulsory; a title; welfare areas on non-welfare questions |
| Tariff / trade | World price line, tariff-inclusive price, domestic S/D; effects on price, production, consumption, expenditure, revenue, welfare per the prescribed diagram (S1) | Which of the effects must be indicated for the question asked | Every area shaded on every question; a title |
| Short-run cost curves (HL) | Cost curves (e.g. AC/MC) against output; standard shapes (S1, S2 mentions average-cost reasoning) | Which curves and relationships the question requires | Market equilibrium or supply/demand shifts (different family); a title |

## 5. Why Diagram Evidence V1 remains feedback-only

Markschemes judge a diagram as "correct" against **question-specific requirement lists**
written by examiners for that exact question (S2). Aptly sees arbitrary pasted questions, has
no per-question requirement list, and vision review of hand-drawn photos is not yet calibrated
against examiner judgments. Awarding or adjusting marks from a photo would therefore manufacture
precision the evidence does not support — precisely what Aptly's integrity rules forbid. The
current design already matches the verified band structure conservatively: with no assessable
diagram, a 4-mark diagram-explain answer is capped at the written component (max 2), which is
exactly the ceiling of the "explanation only" 1–2 band (S2). Feedback-only review adds value
(readability, relevance, element observations, consistency with the written answer) without
claiming marking authority, and every card carries the not-IB / no-mark-effect limitation.

## 6. Requirements for any future diagram-marking V2

Before Aptly could ever award the diagram component (e.g. lift the 4-mark cap):
1. A per-question requirements model equivalent to the markscheme's enumerated diagram
   requirements — derived reliably from the question text alone, validated against real
   markschemes across sessions.
2. A calibration study: vision verdicts vs examiner-marked diagrams (real hand-drawn photos),
   with published error rates for each band decision, including the mislabel cap.
3. Faithful reproduction of the OR/AND band logic — never additive part-marks a markscheme
   doesn't use — with the mislabel cap enforced.
4. Conservative failure behaviour: any uncertainty falls back to today's feedback-only cap
   (never upward), preserving `scoringState` semantics and core-analytics exclusion.
5. All existing protections re-verified: text-only `/api/grade` unchanged for written marks,
   transient images, no analytics contamination, and explicit product copy that estimates
   remain unofficial.

Until every item is met, the cap stays and diagram review stays feedback-only.
